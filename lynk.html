Telemetry Service at Lynk Global

My Role

As a full-stack-minded product designer, I led the development of a telemetry service from scratch during my internship at Lynk Global. My mission: blend technical rigor with strategic design to support satellite engineers with a fast, reliable, and transparent data interface—building not just tools, but trust across teams.

The Problem

Legacy telemetry relied on static hex dumps, manual cronjobs, and CSV parsing—creating barriers for engineers trying to monitor spacecraft health in real time. There was no easy way to search, visualize, or trust the data without digging through text files.

Design Process

Inspired by agile methods, I took a systems-first approach, progressing through four key stages:

Service Design: Diagrammed architecture (subscriber, database, query API) with clarity and modularity in mind.

Strategic Stack Selection: Chose Rust, gRPC, PostgreSQL, and Protobuf to ensure precision, safety, and scalability.

Implementation: Developed a CLI-based query layer and backend from scratch—tested and optimized for real-time use.

Iteration & Feedback: Incorporated usability suggestions from flight and ground teams to streamline workflows.

Outcome

The new system transformed telemetry access from file-based guesswork to live, networked insight. Engineers could now query health metrics in real time, track spacecraft events, and debug failures faster—without learning a new language. My work laid the groundwork for GUI expansion and cross-team adoption.

Learnings

This project reminded me that backend work can—and should—be user-centered. I learned how to bring strategy to systems design: how to balance speed with flexibility, precision with usability, and solo coding with stakeholder empathy. Even deep in the terminal, I was designing for people. That’s what excites me most.
