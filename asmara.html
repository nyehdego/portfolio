Asmara Restaurant

My Role

I conducted a responsive redesign for the menu page of my mother's restaurant, Asmara Restaurant - a project that combined product design and accessibility. As a product designer passionate about blending tech, strategy, and community, I aimed to modernize the site with making it more accessibile, learnable, and memorable.

The Problem

The original page was outdated, difficult to navigate on mobile, and didn’t reflect the warmth of the restaurant. Users struggled to find basic info like hours and menu, and the layout didn’t scale across devices.

Design Process

I followed a structured solo design process, beginning with visual standards and working down to screen-specific mockups before building the final responsive site. Open images in a new tab to see them in full!

Style Guide: Defined fonts, color palette, and iconography to reflect the restaurant’s Eritrean warmth and history.

Desktop Layout: Crafted a clean visual hierarchy with clear buttons, accessible type, and active/hover capabilties.

Tablet Layout: Adapted the design to mid-sized screens, maintaining readability and intuitive structure across breakpoints.

Mobile Layout: Reorganized content for quick scanning—prioritizing menu access, contact info, and navigation clarity.

Outcome

The redesign improved mobile accessibility and user clarity while staying true to the restaurant’s identity. The new site is readable, responsive, and easier to navigate. I prioritized key info like food categories while adding cultural touches through typography and visuals.

Learnings

This project reinforced my belief that good design is about clarity, culture, and care. I learned to think in terms of device breakpoints, semantic HTML, and content hierarchy—all while elevating my mom's small business I care deeply about.
