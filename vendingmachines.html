Vending Machine Personas

My Role

As a solo researcher and designer, I observed real user behavior, conducted interviews, and synthesized insights into personas and storyboards over vending machines, a popular public interface. This project helped me practice the kind of community-rooted, creative thinking I bring to every product design challenge.

The Problem

Vending machines serve many students a day—but their design often creates confusion, inefficiencies, or missed expectations. I set out to understand how people interact with them and how those interactions could inform more thoughtful design.

Research Process

I approached the project in four phases:

Field Observation: Documented student interactions at a vending machine—timing use, noting errors, and capturing behavioral patterns.

Interviews: Asked students about their experiences, frustrations, and expectations to surface deeper motivations.

First Persona: Developed a user persona representing the rushed student.

Second Persona: Developed a user persona representing the rushed student the curious snack explorer.

Storyboard

To bring the user journey to life, I created a nine-panel storyboard illustrating one persona's experience—capturing their thoughts, emotions, and actions throughout the vending machine interaction.

Learnings

This project reminded me that even the most mundane systems—like vending machines—hold design opportunities when approached with empathy. By slowing down and listening to users, I practiced one of my core values as a designer: blending tech, strategy, and community insight to make daily experiences better.
